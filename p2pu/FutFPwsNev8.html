Recap:

Strategy:  P2PU - the open learning hub (paper)

  • "Mission - P2PU is the resource hub and a community that helps more people build peer to peer learning experiences online. We bring together and support practitioners, who believe in open, peer learning and community. We give them the tools, advice, and support they need to build great social learning projects online. 
  • ...
  • At P2PU you find:
    • information & inspiration: best practices and how-tos, reports and showcase projects to inspire and demonstrate what is possible  
    • a collection of well documented open software tools and applications, 
    • courses on open social learning, to help more practitioners get started, 
    • a community of helpful practitioners willing to share and help, and 
    • a collaboration partner for showcase projects, research, and consulting.

  • ...
  • We do > Run a great website, inspire and inform, provide know-how and a list of tools (and how to use them), maintain a great community that supports each other, offer courses on all aspects of open social learning, and provide advocacy. 
  • ...
  • We might > Partner with others to create showcase courses or schools on other topics, do research, or offer more extensive advice/assistance as consulting services. 
  • ...

Other issues

- future of courses/lernanta:

  • a course on designing open courses?
    • slide deck? other stuff?
  • has to be preserved in some form; won’t delete content
  • for active schools -> leave for another discussion (no question they will remain core to ’what is P2PU’)
  • for inactive schools / 1-off and inactive courses -> some options:
    1. we maintain lernanta, as a tool, not the tool, eg ’if you want to host a course and don’t know where to begin, check out our lernanta project, a relatively simple (not under active development) but robust option for delivering smaller courses’
      1. if we don’t maintain it and it’s not good enough, we shouldn’t point people there.
      2. also, it will only get worse (and make us look old-fashioned)
      3. there’s a backlog of stuff to be archived; consensus is the older stuff should be archived
      4. can plan for next 6 months to turn off the ’create a course’ function
      5. then think about how we package that going forward
    2. we migrate everything to someone else’s platform (is this realistic? would we want to truly abandon all record of the time, money and community effort invested to date?)
    3. other options?
      1. blogs
      2. github pages
      3. wiki
      4. archive everything we have as read-only, but not allow creation of new courses

  • hopefully no big objections - change to be respectful, additive, helpful (see ’next steps’)
  • grumbles might be different to objections
